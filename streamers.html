Streamer Fundraising Collaborations with NARBE Foundation

Join a small group of creators raising awareness and support for accessible gaming and communication through community-powered streams.

What this is about

We’re inviting a small group of streamers to collaborate with us on fundraising events that raise awareness for people like my brother Ben, who is nonverbal and quadriplegic due to a rare condition called TUBB4A leukodystrophy.

Through the NARBE Foundation, we build simple adaptive games and communication tools using AI and share them with families for free. Our goal is to show how creativity and technology can restore joy, play, and connection — and to teach others how to do it too.

Why it might be cool for you

  • Be part of something that directly impacts real families and lives.
  • The foundation is partnered with our family collective, NARBE House (@narbehouse), with over 140,000 followers across YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram — we’ll promote your streams and highlight your community’s impact.
  • Streamers who participate will be featured on our website and socials, with clips, thank-yous, and ongoing recognition.
  • Fun incentives and milestones tailored for your platform to celebrate fundraising success.
  • Creative freedom — gaming, art, chatting, or storytelling, it all makes a difference.

How it works

  • Pick a day to stream for the cause.
  • We’ll provide overlays, donation links, and visuals.
  • You stream your way — games, art, discussion, or collab events.
  • We’ll amplify your stream through both NARBE Foundation and NARBE House channels.
  • After the event, we’ll share highlights, thank-yous, and impact clips.

What we’re building

  • A community to create accessible games and tools — like one or two-button games for people who can’t use traditional controllers.
  • Free and low-cost communication and entertainment systems shared openly.
  • Tutorials and guides that teach families how to build adaptive tech using AI.
  • Demonstrations and competitions for accessible game creation, with prize pools.
  • Future initiatives to acquire and donate adaptive hardware to families in need.
